The Hinge Doorstop

Open the door. Set it. It holds — wide open, hands-free, nothing on the floor.

The problem

Propping heavy doors costs your CA's time — and breaks things.

  • Garbage cans get fetched from inside each unit, then the heavy door wears them out.
  • Wedge doorstops grind against the bottom of the door under its full weight — scuffing, scoring, and wearing down the finish over time — and they sit out in the walkway as a trip hazard.
  • Either way, staff bend down at every single door to move them. And carry linens and supplies over and around them.
The solution

It lives in the hinge.

No friction. Nothing on the floor. The crew opens the door, drops it into the hinge, and the door stays wide open — off just as fast, no bending over to retrieve. Because it seats in the hinge, metal to metal, it never touches the door's surface: no scuffing, scoring, or wearing down the bottom the way a wedge does.

How it's made

Printed in batches, delivered as they're ready.

Each build plate holds 37 doorstops. A full 500-unit order is 14 plates — roughly 160 hours of printing, about 9 days start to finish. Because the run comes off the printer plate by plate, you don't have to wait for all 500 at once.
